How does a search engine work?
When we search something on a search engine like Google, it analyzes and outputs the result through its own algorithms. To provide the most useful information, the search algorithms look at various factors, such as the words of your query, relevence and usability of pages, expertise of sources, and your location or settings.
First, to show the relevant results for your query, the algorithm must first know what information you are looking for. Understanding the user's intentions is fundamentally understanding the language and this is extremely important in search. Search engines use language models to analyze this. This includes, for example, the spelling mistakes we often go through. Suppose you wanted to search for bananas, but you had butterfingers so that you entered bannana! Nevertheless, the analyzer will read your intentions and bring you bananas admirably.
The algorithm then analyzes the contents of the page to see if it contains information that may be relevant to what you want. For example, if you searched for a banana, it will check if the pages contain the same keyword as the banana, and determine that the page containing the keyword in the heading is highly relevant.
Next, the algorithm will look at the reliability of the source through a system that determines whether the site has expertise and authority on a given topic.
The accessibility of the page is also evaluated when ranking the results according to the search engine. There is a high probability that the site will be displayed correctly in different browsers and in various devices, including a smartphone or tablet.
Why do different search-engines give different ranking to websites for the same search phrase?
Because each search engine has different algorithms.
First, they deal with keywords differently. If you use targeted keywords on your website, your web will be ranked higher on Bing than Google. Because Google uses algorithms that can understand the context behind each search query, Bing still uses targeted keywords as ranking parameters.
Second, whether search engines include meta keywords is different. Bing considers meta keywords as an important ranking factor, but Google has stated that they do not.
Third, the way backlinks are handled is not the same. While Google considers the quality of the backlinks important, Bing looks at both the quantity and quality. If you have a lot of backlinks from a website in a less reliable domain, Google won't trust your website very much.
Fourth, it depends on the importance of social media. A good social media portfolio is advantageous in Bing but not significantly important at Google.
Finally, what content is used on the website is matters. The more text you write on a site, the more Google places it on top. Bing, on the other hand, also includes visual contents such as images and video data in its algorithms, so the more diverse the content it has, the better.
What is a <meta /> tag?
Meta tag is pieces of text that describes the contents of a page, which does not appear on the page itself but appears only in the source. It serves to tell the search engine what pages are about. The difference between metatag and other tags is the only location. Mata tags are located in HTML and are usually found in the head, so they can only be seen in the search engines. You can increase the traffic on your site by providing information about your page to search engines through meta tags.
What is the purpose for Keyword and Description meta tags?
The meta keyword is intended to represent the main keywords of the page, which are not visible to visitors but are visible to search engines. In the past, search engines used it as an important ranking factor, but as there are more spammers nowadays, they no longer consider it an important factor.
The meta description is designed to provide a brief summary of what is found on the web page. The description tag, compared with the title tag that is difficult to put a long explanation, can be a bit longer. This is exposed to a search engine and then can appeal to the users to click your website.
